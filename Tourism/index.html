Famous Heritage Sites

Albert hall

Built in 1876 to commemorate the visit of Prince Albert Edward of Wales (later King Edward VII), Albert Hall was designed by Sir Samuel Swinton Jacob. Initially planned as a town hall, it was later converted into a museum and officially opened to the public in 1887 by Maharaja Sawai Madho Singh II.

Jai Mahal

Jal Mahal is a stunning 18th-century palace built by Maharaja Madho Singh I, located in the middle of Man Sagar Lake. Showcasing Rajput and Mughal architecture, the palace appears to float on water, with four floors submerged when the lake is full, making it one of Jaipur’s most scenic landmarks.

Hawa Mahal

Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, Hawa Mahal—also called the Palace of Winds—is made of red and pink sandstone. With 953 jharokha windows, it allows cool air to circulate and reflects Rajput architecture, enabling royal women to observe street life while remaining unseen.
