Hawa Mahal (Jaipur), also known as the Palace of Winds, was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. Made of red and pink sandstone, it features 953 small windows (jharokhas) designed to allow cool air to circulate. The palace reflects Rajput architecture and was built for royal women to observe street life while remaining unseen.
