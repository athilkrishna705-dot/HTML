Albert Hall was built in 1876 to commemorate the visit of Prince Albert Edward of Wales (later King Edward VII) to India. The building was designed by Sir Samuel Swinton Jacob, a British architect. Originally, the structure was planned as a town hall, but later it was converted into a museum. The museum was officially opened to the public in 1887 by Maharaja Sawai Madho Singh II.
